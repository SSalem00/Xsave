# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: June 2026**

XSave is a browser extension that downloads media from X (Twitter). This policy explains what it does and does not do with your data.

## What XSave does not do

- XSave does not collect any personal data.
- XSave does not track your browsing activity.
- XSave does not send any information to any external server or third party.
- XSave does not use analytics, telemetry, or crash reporting.
- XSave does not store anything beyond your current browser session.

## What XSave does

When you click the download button on a tweet, XSave:

1. Fetches the media URL from X's own public CDN using your browser — the same request your browser would make anyway to display the tweet.
2. For GIFs: decodes and re-encodes the video locally in your browser using an embedded encoder. This process happens entirely on your device.
3. Saves the resulting file to your computer via your browser's built-in download system.

No data leaves your device except the requests made directly to X's servers to retrieve the media you asked to download.
